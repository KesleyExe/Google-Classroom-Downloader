Step 1: Go to Google Cloud Console
- Open your browser and go to:
https://console.cloud.google.com/
- Sign in with the same Google account you use for Google Classroom.


Step 2: Create a New Project
- At the very top of the page, click the dropdown that says "Select a project"
- Click "New Project" in the popup
- Give it a name:
- Project name: Classroom Downloader
- Leave "Location" as default
- Click "Create"
- Wait a few seconds, then select your new project from the dropdown


Step 3: Enable the APIs
- Google Classroom and Google Drive APIs are off by default. You need to turn them on:
- In the left sidebar, click "APIs & Services" → "Library"
- In the search bar, type: "Google Classroom API"
- Click on "Google Classroom API" in the results
- Click the blue "Enable" button
- Wait for it to finish (you'll see a green checkmark)
- Go back to the Library (click the back arrow or search again)
- Search for: "Google Drive API"
- Click on "Google Drive API"
- Click the blue "Enable" button


Step 4: Configure the OAuth Consent Screen
- This is the "Sign in with Google" popup that appears when you run the tool:
- In the left sidebar, click "APIs & Services" → "OAuth consent screen"
- Under "User Type", select "External"
- This means anyone with a Google account can use it (including you)
- Click "Create"
- Fill in the app information:
- App name: Classroom Downloader
- User support email: Your email address (pick from dropdown)
- App logo: Skip this (optional)
- App domain: Leave blank
- Developer contact information: Your email address
- Click "Save and Continue"
- On the "Scopes" screen:
- Click "Add or Remove Scopes"
- In the filter box, search for each of these and check the box:
.../auth/classroom.courses.readonly
.../auth/classroom.courseworkmaterials.readonly
.../auth/classroom.coursework.students.readonly
.../auth/classroom.announcements.readonly
.../auth/drive.readonly
- Click "Update" at the bottom
- Click "Save and Continue"
- On the "Test users" screen:
- Click "+ Add Users"
- Type your email address
- Click "Add"
- Click "Save and Continue"
- On the "Summary" screen, just click "Back to Dashboard"
- Important: Your app stays in "Testing" mode. That's fine for personal use. Don't click "Publish" unless you want strangers to use your app (which requires a privacy policy and Google verification).

Step 5: Create Your Credentials File
- Now you create the actual credentials.json file:
- In the left sidebar, click "APIs & Services" → "Credentials"
- https://i.imgur.com/placeholder4.png
- At the top, click "+ Create Credentials"
- Select "OAuth 2.0 Client ID"
- On the next screen:
- Application type: Select "Desktop app"
- ⚠️ This is important! Don't pick "Web application" or "Android"
- Name: Classroom Downloader Desktop
- Click "Create"
- A popup appears showing your Client ID and Client Secret
- Don't close this yet!
- Click "Download JSON" (the blue button)
- Save the file to your computer (probably goes to Downloads)

Step 6: Put the File in Your Project Folder
- Find the downloaded file in your Downloads folder
- It will be named something like client_secret_123456789-abc123.apps.googleusercontent.com.json
Rename it to exactly: 
    credentials.json
Move it to your project folder:
    C:\Users\Test\Downloads\Google Classroom Downloader\
Your folder should now look like this:
    Google Classroom Downloader/
    ├── .gitignore
    ├── README.md
    ├── requirements.txt
    ├── main.py
    └── credentials.json    ← You just added this

Step 7: Verify It Works
Open PowerShell in your project folder and run:
    powershell
    py main.py
If everything worked:
    A browser tab opens
    You sign in with Google
    You might see a warning — click "Advanced" → "Go to Classroom Downloader (unsafe)"
    Click "Allow" for the permissions
    Return to PowerShell
    You should see your courses listed!

Common Mistakes
- Picked "Web application" instead of "Desktop app"	
- Delete the credential, create a new one with "Desktop app"
- Forgot to enable both APIs	Go back to Library, search for both, click Enable
- Didn't add myself as a test user	Go to OAuth consent screen → Test users → Add your email
- File is still named client_secret_...json	Rename it to exactly credentials.json
- Put it in the wrong folder	
- Make sure it's in the same folder as main.py
